In the quiet chaos of a summer afternoon, a lone duck floats gently on the surface of a pool, seemingly unaffected by the world around it. Its feathers glisten under the sun, ripples trailing behind with every calm stroke. The scene should feel peaceful—but beneath the surface, another story unfolds.

In the distance, muffled voices clash—sharp, emotional, unresolved. A couple arguing, their words cutting through the still air like sudden cracks of thunder. Just beyond them, laughter hums from children playing nearby. Their innocent melodies and splashes echo off the water, softening the tension but never fully silencing it.

The duck drifts on, unbothered, a silent observer to this strange contrast of tension and joy. It glides between worlds—one of innocence and one of emotional storm—each existing side by side in a single, fleeting moment.

Life continues in fragments: conflict, joy, silence. And in the center of it all, the duck simply floats—calm, steady, present.
